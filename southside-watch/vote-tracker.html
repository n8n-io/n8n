How to Attend and Participate

Every public body listed above holds regular public meetings. Zoning decisions and tax incentive approvals require public hearings where residents have the right to speak.

  • Find upcoming meetings

    Check each county's or city's official website for meeting calendars. Sign up for agendas if notification is offered.

  • Request the full agenda package

    Agenda packets often contain supporting documents, staff reports, and agreement drafts. Request these before the meeting — they may not be posted publicly.

  • Sign up to speak during public comment

    Most public hearings allow residents to speak for 3–5 minutes. Prepare specific questions or concerns in writing.

  • Bring others

    Attendance matters. A packed room of engaged residents sends a message. Coordinate with neighbors and community organizations.

  • Document everything

    Record public meetings (most are permitted under Virginia law). Submit your recordings and notes to us for the public record.
