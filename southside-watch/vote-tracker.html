How to Attend and Be Heard

  • Find meeting calendars

    Each county's and city's official website lists meeting schedules. Most boards meet monthly. Zoning hearings often scheduled separately.

  • Request the full agenda packet

    Agenda packets contain staff reports, application materials, and sometimes agreement drafts. Request these before the meeting — email the county clerk or administrator.

  • Sign up to speak

    Public hearings allow residents to speak, usually 3–5 minutes. Prepare specific questions in writing. Focus on unanswered questions, not opinions.

  • Bring neighbors and document everything

    Attendance sends a message. Record meetings (permitted under Virginia law for public meetings). Share your recordings with us.

  • Submit a FOIA request

    If key documents aren't in the packet, file a written FOIA request to the FOIA Officer. Virginia agencies must respond within 5 business days.
