About Southside Virginia Data Center Watch

A public-interest transparency project built by and for the residents of Southside Virginia.

Why This Project Exists

Southside Virginia — Emporia, Greensville County, Brunswick County, South Hill, Franklin, Southampton County, Mecklenburg County, Sussex County, and the surrounding communities — has historically been one of the most economically distressed regions of the Commonwealth. These communities have seen jobs leave, infrastructure age, and populations decline for decades.

Data center development is now arriving in this region, attracted by available land, relatively lower costs, and increasing proximity to the Northern Virginia data center corridor. Developers and officials are presenting these projects as economic revitalization — promising jobs, tax revenue, and investment.

Economic development matters. Jobs matter. Tax revenue matters.

But so does accountability. And so far, the record in Southside Virginia has shown a troubling pattern: projects approved without adequate public notice, incentive agreements withheld from the public before votes, ownership structures hidden behind LLCs, and fundamental questions about energy costs and water impact left unanswered.

Southside Virginia Data Center Watch exists to change that.

Our Mission

We compile, publish, and analyze public information about data center development across Southside Virginia. We track every public vote, every incentive deal, every permit application, and every community impact. We make this information accessible to every resident, whether or not they can attend a Tuesday morning board meeting.

We are not anti-technology. We are not anti-development. We believe economic investment in long-underserved communities is genuinely important — and that it must be done transparently, fairly, and with real accountability to the people who live there.

Our Standards

Source Transparency

Every factual claim on this site is sourced. We distinguish between verified public records (high confidence), community reports under investigation (medium/low confidence), and analysis or inference (clearly labeled as such). We do not publish rumors as facts.

Corrections Policy

If we have something wrong, we want to know. Submit a correction via our Report form and we will review it promptly. We publish corrections clearly when errors are confirmed. We do not silently edit or delete corrections.

Independence

We accept no funding from data center developers, utility companies, real estate interests, or any entity with a financial stake in the projects we cover. We are not affiliated with any political party or advocacy organization. Our only interest is that Southside Virginia communities have access to accurate, complete public information.

Source Protection

We protect the identity of community sources and whistleblowers. If you provide information to us in confidence, we will never reveal your identity without your explicit consent. We do not track or record identifying metadata beyond what is necessary to respond to your report.

What We Are Not

We are not a legal service and do not provide legal advice. We are not a government agency. We are not affiliated with any elected official or political party. We are not a lobbying organization. We do not endorse or oppose specific candidates or officials.

We believe in local government — we want it to work well, which is why we hold it accountable.

Get Involved

This project grows with community participation. You can help by:

  • Submitting tips, documents, and reports via our Report a Concern form
  • Attending local board meetings and sharing recordings
  • Filing FOIA requests and sharing the results
  • Sharing this site with your neighbors, your local newspaper, and your county representative
  • Correcting errors or adding missing information
