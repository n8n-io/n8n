☑ Resident Checklist

Questions Every Resident Should Ask Before Any Data Center Is Approved

Bring this checklist to public hearings. Ask your county supervisor. Request answers in writing before any vote. These questions are not anti-development — they are what responsible governance looks like.

🏢 Ownership & Identity

  • Who is the developer? What is their full legal name, parent company, and history of projects in Virginia?
  • Who are the beneficial owners of the developer entity? If it's an LLC, who owns it? Who controls it?
  • Who is the end user — the company whose servers will occupy the facility? Amazon? Microsoft? Google? Someone else?
  • Is there an NDA that prevents disclosure of the developer or end user before the vote? If so, why is the community expected to approve something without knowing who they're approving it for?
  • What is the developer's track record? Have they built similar projects elsewhere? What happened in those communities?

⚡ Power & Energy

  • How many megawatts will the project require? At Phase 1? At full buildout? What is the maximum possible demand?
  • Who pays for grid upgrades? Is there a binding commitment from the developer — or will costs be passed to ratepayers?
  • Has Dominion Energy or the relevant utility filed any documents with the SCC regarding transmission upgrades for this project?
  • Will residents' electric bills be affected? Has the utility provided an independent assessment?
  • How many backup diesel generators will be installed? What is their total generating capacity?
  • What is the testing schedule for backup generators? How long will they run? At what times of day?
  • What air quality permits are required for the generators? Have they been obtained or applied for?
  • What emission controls will be installed on generators to protect nearby residents?

💧 Water & Sewer

  • What cooling system will be used? Wet cooling towers? Dry cooling? Hybrid? Reclaimed water?
  • How much water will be used daily? At Phase 1? At full buildout? What is the maximum possible demand?
  • Has the local water authority confirmed it has capacity to serve this project without impacting existing customers?
  • Who pays for water system upgrades if the existing infrastructure cannot support the project?
  • What is the source of water supply? Municipal system? Groundwater wells? Surface water?
  • How will the project handle drought conditions? Is there a drought contingency plan?
  • Is there a wastewater plan? Where will cooling tower blowdown and other process water be discharged?
  • Has the sewer system been assessed for capacity? Who pays for upgrades if needed?

💰 Tax Incentives & Public Money

  • What tax breaks are being offered? PILOT? Abatement? Enterprise zone credits? Grants?
  • What is the total estimated value of all incentives over their full term, in today's dollars?
  • Will the full agreement be released publicly at least 30 days before any vote?
  • Is any public infrastructure grant being awarded? From what fund? What is the public interest justification?
  • Are there clawback provisions if the developer fails to meet job or investment commitments?
  • What independent fiscal impact analysis has been done? Who conducted it? Is it available to the public?
  • After all incentives, how much net tax revenue will the county actually receive in Year 1? Year 5? Year 10?

👷 Jobs & Local Economy

  • How many permanent local jobs will be created? Not construction jobs — permanent, full-time jobs.
  • What is the salary range for those jobs? Are they living-wage jobs accessible to local residents?
  • Will there be a local hiring requirement? Is it binding and enforceable?
  • Will local residents be trained and hired? Is there a partnership with a local community college or trade school?
  • What is the cost per job when all public subsidies are accounted for?
  • What is the penalty if promised jobs are not created or maintained?
  • Are there local vendor or procurement requirements for construction and ongoing operations?

🌿 Environment & Community

  • Has an environmental impact assessment been completed? Is it available to the public?
  • Is the site in or near a floodplain, wetland, or watershed? What studies have been done?
  • What noise levels are expected from cooling equipment and backup generators? Have decibel measurements been provided?
  • What is the construction traffic plan? Which roads will be used? What hours? Who pays for road damage?
  • What are the stormwater management plans for the site during and after construction?
  • What is the decommissioning plan if the facility closes? Who is responsible for cleanup and site remediation?
  • Have impacted residential neighbors been individually notified and given the opportunity to comment?

📋 Process & Transparency

  • Will the full agreement be released before the vote? How far in advance?
  • Was adequate public notice given? State law minimums are a floor, not a standard.
  • Has the rezoning request been reviewed in the context of the comprehensive plan?
  • Will there be an opportunity for meaningful public comment — not just a check-the-box hearing?
  • Is there a community benefit agreement? Who negotiated it? Who represents the community?
  • Will there be annual public reporting on jobs, revenue, water use, and compliance with all conditions?
  • Who can residents contact if conditions are violated after approval?

💡 How to Use This Checklist

  • Print it and bring it to public hearings
  • Ask your county supervisor to respond in writing
  • Share it with neighbors and local journalists
  • Record public meetings and share the recordings
  • Submit unanswered questions as FOIA requests

⚠ Remember

These questions are not anti-development. Economic development that is transparent, fairly compensated, and genuinely beneficial to local communities is worth supporting. The goal is accountability — not obstruction.
