Virginia FOIA: Your Right to Public Records

The Virginia Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) gives you the right to access public records from state and local government bodies. This includes meeting minutes, voting records, permit applications, incentive agreements, and correspondence related to data center projects.

How to File a FOIA Request

  • Identify the custodian

    Determine which government body has the records you need (county administrator's office, building department, EDA, etc.). Each agency has a designated FOIA officer.

  • Write your request

    Be specific: describe what you're looking for by date range, subject, or document type. Include your contact information.

  • Send in writing

    Email is accepted. Send to the FOIA officer with a clear subject line. Keep a copy for your records.

  • Know the deadline

    Virginia agencies must respond within 5 business days. They may take up to 7 additional days with notification. They cannot ignore your request.

  • Appeal if denied

    If records are improperly withheld, you can file a complaint with the Virginia FOIA Council or pursue legal remedies in circuit court.

Sample FOIA Language: "I request all records, including but not limited to correspondence, memoranda, agreements, draft agreements, meeting minutes, and permit applications, related to [Project Name] or [Developer Name] from [Start Date] to present."
