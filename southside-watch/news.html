Brunswick Co. Board Approves Apex Digital 120 MW — 4-1; PILOT Agreement Still Withheld

The Brunswick County Board of Supervisors voted 4-1 to approve the Brunswick Crossroads Data Center and a 15-year PILOT agreement. Supervisor James Watkins cast the sole dissent, citing unresolved sewer capacity and the fact that the full PILOT text was not available for public review before the vote. A $8.2M EDA infrastructure grant was also approved. As of May 2025, the full PILOT agreement remains withheld — a FOIA appeal is pending after the county cited an exemption on the initial request.

South Hill / Mecklenburg Process Failure
Sep 15, 2024

Water Authority Capacity Warning Kept Out of Board Packet — Permit Issued Anyway

An internal memo from the South Hill Water Authority, obtained via FOIA, shows officials warned on August 12, 2024 that the system was at 74% capacity and estimated a $4.1 million upgrade would be needed to serve the Piedmont Data Centers Phase 1 load. This memo was not included in the Board of Supervisors meeting packet for the September 15 approval vote. The Board voted 5-0 to approve without discussion of water capacity. Phase 2 could double the demand. The water upgrade cost has not been publicly resolved.

Southampton / SCC Verified — SCC Docket
Jun 18, 2024

$29.4M Dominion Transmission Upgrade in Southampton County — Cost Socialized to All Ratepayers

Virginia SCC Case PUR-2024-00078 documents Dominion Energy's approval to build a 138 kV transmission upgrade in Southampton County, citing anticipated load growth from unnamed industrial customers. The $29.4 million project cost was approved for recovery from all Dominion customers across the service territory — not charged directly to data center developers whose load growth required the upgrade. The Virginia Citizens Consumer Council filed an intervener objection on cost allocation grounds; it was overruled. No Southside Virginia local government was notified before the SCC filing.

Your Rights

How to File a Virginia FOIA Request

The Virginia Freedom of Information Act gives you the right to access public records from state and local government bodies. This includes meeting minutes, voting records, permit applications, incentive agreements, and all correspondence related to data center projects.

Sample request language: "I request all records, including but not limited to correspondence, memoranda, agreements, draft agreements, meeting minutes, staff reports, and permit applications, related to [Project Name / Developer Name] from [Start Date] to present. Please provide records in electronic form where possible."
  • Identify the custodian

    Determine which body holds the records (county administrator, planning department, EDA, building department). Each agency has a designated FOIA officer.

  • Send your request in writing (email is fine)

    Be specific: date ranges, document types, project names, developer names. Keep a copy with the timestamp you sent it.

  • Know the legal deadline

    Virginia agencies must respond within 5 business days. They may take 7 additional days with written notification. Silence is not acceptable.

  • If denied, appeal

    File a petition with the Virginia FOIA Council (free, online), or pursue legal action in circuit court. Wrongful withholding can result in attorney fee awards.
